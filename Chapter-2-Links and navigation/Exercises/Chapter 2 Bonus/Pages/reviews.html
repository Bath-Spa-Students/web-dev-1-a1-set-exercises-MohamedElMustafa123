Matthew Mussett

This 2018 American computer-animated superhero film is one of the most solid animations which aren't made by Disney or Pixar in a long, long time. Here's my reasons for why this film deserves all the accolades it has received. Without a doubt, the best part of this film is it's animation. It's the combined talents of 140 animators all working to mix computer-animation with traditional band-drawn comic book techniques which makes this film a literal sight to behold. The characters are so engaging, but none are quite like Miles Morales. Before, to be honest, he was basically black Spider-Man to even the most hardcore of comic book fans. But now, thanks to this film, he is a far more fleshed-out and developed character with real motivations and relatability. The story is also complex and real. The amount of attention put into the writing has made this story so believable, despite easily tackling concepts such as multiple realities and super-powered individuals. The voice acting is on point. In this film, shout out to Jake Johnson, who's portrayal as an old and experienced Peter Parker who hasn't just been through life as Spider-Man but also just the toils of life in general is so entertaining and also interesting. It's a new look on an iconic character which we’ve never seen before, yet love all the same. To an extent, the unique aesthetics themselves allow for a few laughs here and there. But there is so much comedy in this fairly emotional rollercoaster of a film that it's hard to even describe. From personal gags between characters to jokes based on the lives of New York citizens, every funny moment got at least a giggle out of me. Finally, the soundtrack is on point. Having listened to the album on Spotify multiple times, you can see that many of the songs relate to Miles' struggle. Each song somehow carefully reflects a key aspect of the film, whether blatantly obvious or a little harder to realise. Also, the genres the songs fall into reflect Miles’ urban upbringing and culture which is really something for an animated film. Overall, nothing I could say could do this film justice. This is a film that me and so many other comic book fans have been waiting for and oh boy, we were in for a treat. This film was dedicated to the memories of the creators of Spider-Man, Stan Lee and Steve Ditko, and this film couldn’t have honoured them in any better way.

The Ultimate Spider-Man Movie of All Time! The movie is an outstanding piece of art that would have certainly received approval from Stan Lee himself. Its animation is incredible, and the story is both great and thought-provoking. The film not only lives up to the hype but also exceeds expectations, despite some negative reviews from viewers who cannot be satisfied no matter what. In general, the vast majority of viewers are likely to find the film to be amazing, especially when watched in theaters, as it features an extraordinary animation style. Every aspect of the movie, such as visuals, concepts, soundtrack, characters, pace, humor, and animation, is among the best ever seen in any movie. It is visually engaging, beautiful in every scene, and features an incredibly unique art style, which sets a high bar for other animated movies. The flashing lights may alienate epileptic audiences, but it is done in an artistic way. Despite this, the film is almost perfect, and the visuals alone won it an Oscar. The story is great, introducing us to a new Spider-Man with his own unique skills and personality. The script is tight, and the production is executed with precision. The voice acting is top-notch, with Shamiek Moore as Miles Morales and Jake Johnson as Peter Parker delivering superb performances. Hailee Steinfeld and the supporting cast also shine in this movie. As far as continuity/plot holes are concerned, the movie could have been 15-20 minutes longer. Nonetheless, there aren't many issues, and the visuals are the film's main attraction, which can only be fully appreciated by watching it in 3D. In conclusion, the film is an exceptional piece of art that will leave a lasting impression on viewers, as it surpasses every criterion of making a nearly perfect movie.
